Self Confidence

22nd March 2017

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

Mindfulness and self awareness are very similar going by the definitions from this chapter. The two quotes outlining what they are sum it up quite well.

"Self-awareness â€¦ is a neutral mode that maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions." - Daniel Goleman.
"Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally." - Jon Kabat-Zinn.

Both are very similar going by these quotes and the chapter suggests that by practicing mindfulness, it will allow one to train self-awareness. Both espouse a view of mastering the ability to not be dictated to by emotions and to react objectively, and logically (Spock?) to a situation.

Try at least one of the self awareness practices from the chapter (Body Scan, Journaling). What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

I tried body scanning. Not quite sure I got the hang of it. Amusingly, as per the note in the book, I really, really felt like going to sleep while trying it. I couldn't quite focus on my neck and shoulders while trying to do this so don't thnk I really figured it out. I will try it again though and see how I find it.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

I very much like the idea. I probably let my heart rule my head a little too much and I do often find myself taken by emotion when stepping back and looking at something objectively would really really help. I am looking forward to the next chapter and its discussion on dealing with negative emotions.
