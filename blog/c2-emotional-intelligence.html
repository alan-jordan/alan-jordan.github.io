Emotional Intelligence

2nd March 2017

Things I took from the first chapter of Search Inside Yourself

The mind isn’t a fixed ‘thing’. It is something that can be shaped to extract more use from it.

The discussion of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy was very interesting. I have experience with a loved one who has benefitted immensely from CBT.

I also found the notes on physical sensations and emotions interesting. I definitely feel things physically. When very angry, I shake and tense up muscles. When relieved, I can almost feel tension draining away.

My wife and I used to joke about the feeling of tension lifting as we crossed the M25 on a train when leaving London. Living in London can leave you coiled up like a spring. When going away for a weekend, that spring used to gradually relax as I got further away from the city. Disclaimer, I still love London but I don’t miss being a bit wound up all the time.

The two minute of mindfulness idea is interesting. I already do practice something like this at times. I have been trying to stop using my mobile phone so much when on public transport or waiting to meet someone. Instead, I try to just sit there and enjoy the world.

I will definitely try the two minutes of mindfulness technique before sleeping to try and train myself to be more mindful.
